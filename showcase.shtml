Free to all UW-Madison Campus Researchers

Date:Thursday, October 6, 2011
Time:1–3 p.m.
Room:Computer Sciences (CompSci) 1240

Over the past year, 54 research groups across 35 departments here at UW-Madison advanced their research by using a total of 45 MILLION hours of computing time for FREE from our very own Center for High Throughput Computing (CHTC).

In this presentation, three of your UW-Madison colleagues will describe how they have used the CHTC to advance their science. Also, CHTC staff will explain how you can harness some of the free computing power on this campus for your own research; they will outline the services that are offered, how to start working with the CHTC, and where to learn more.

Speakers include:

  • Edgar Spalding, Professor of Botany, UW–Madison
  • Jeff Klukas, Graduate Student in Physics, UW–Madison
  • Jeff Nie, Computational Biologist, Morgridge Institute for Research
